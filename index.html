Dr. Anastasia Klimovich-Gray

Hi! I am a neuroscientist based at BCBL in San Sebastian, Spain. I use neuroimaging to study how our brains process language. I am particularly interested in the flexible adaptation of speech processing in the brain and how this translates into diverse comprehension strategies in typical and atypical populations.

Current Projects

Reading effects on speech perception and produciton

I am working on a ReadCalibraion project headed by Prof. Clara Martin which explores how reading acquisition may change the way we process speech. Check out this recent presentation by Mina Jevtovic at the International Child Phonology Conference 2021

Predictive Processing of Language

Jointly with Dr. Nicola Molinaro in the PreSpeech project we look at how the ability to predict upcoming information shapes cortical speech processing strategies. Here is our recent paper from this project

Exploring Dyslexia with MEG and neural networks

In an exciting collaboration with Prof. Eneko Agirre and Dr. Ander Barrena we are looking at ways to identify presence of dyslexic symptoms from neuroimaging data using neural networks

Bio & Contact

I graduated from SOAS in 2011 with a degree in Linguistics and Chinese. In 2012 I completed an MSc in Linguistics and Neuroscience at the UCL Division of Psychology and Language Sciences. I have received a PhD from the University of Cambridge in 2017, under the supervision fo Prof. William Marslen-Wilson. I then went on to do a postdoc at the Centre for Speech, Language, and the Brain (CSLB) and a short placement at the Cambridge Centre for Frontotemporal Dementia and Related Disorders before joining BCBL as a Marie Curie fellow.
